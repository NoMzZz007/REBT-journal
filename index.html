A
Activating event
What just happened?
Describe the situation like a camera would record it — observable facts, no interpretations yet.
try starting with →
froze on my list
doomscrolled the news
avoidance spiral
did nothing all day
behind before starting
Tip: Write what happened like a security camera would see it — no interpretations yet. "I sat at my desk for an hour without opening the file" is good. "I wasted my day again" has a judgment baked in — save that for B.
B
Beliefs
What were you telling yourself?
The automatic thoughts that appeared. Look for hidden "musts," "shoulds," and absolutes — they often sound reasonable.
common patterns — tap any that fit →
must be productive
should be further along
can't start unless ready
all-or-nothing
world feels hopeless
self-blame loop
need certainty first
comparison spiral
For anxiety + HFD: Your beliefs often sound "realistic." Watch for thoughts like "given the economy, nothing I do matters" — the first part may be true, but "nothing I do matters" is the irrational absolute hiding inside it. That's the part to work with.
C
Consequence
What did you feel and do?
Be specific — anxiety feels different from dread, which feels different from numbness. Name the behavior too.
physical + behavioral →
chest tightness
flat / hollow
task-switching
phone escape
body tension
stayed in bed
Two types of negative emotions: Healthy ones — disappointment, frustration, concern — are proportionate and can motivate action. Unhealthy ones — dread, shame, paralysis, despair — are amplified by the beliefs in B. Which is closer to what you felt?
D
Disputing
Now, question the belief.
Not toxic positivity. Honest cross-examination. You don't have to win — just find the cracks.
1. Is it actually true? What's the real evidence for and against this belief?
2. Is it helping? Does holding this belief make you more or less able to function right now?
3. Would you say this to a friend? If someone you cared about came to you with this same situation, what would you tell them?
For procrastination paralysis: The belief "I can't start until I feel ready" is the classic trap — because anxiety means the ready feeling never fully arrives. Has waiting for readiness ever made it come? Or does starting — even badly — come first, and the feeling follows?
E
New belief
What's a more flexible belief?
Not "everything is fine." Replace absolute demands with preferences. Swap "must" for "prefer." Swap "can't" for "it's hard but possible."
flexible reframes →
one imperfect start
small action available
don't need to feel ready
hard ≠ impossible
I've been here before
grieve and still move
The key word is "prefer." "I prefer to be productive" keeps the value — you do care about output — but removes the demand that reality must comply. The suffering mostly lives in that demand, not in the value underneath it.
F
Next action
What's one small next action?
Not a plan. Not a solution. The smallest concrete thing you could do in the next 10 minutes — even if it feels too small to matter.
small enough to actually do →
open the thing
write one sentence
5-minute rule
step outside
close the tabs
reach out to someone
For high-functioning depression: Small actions aren't about output — they're about restoring your sense of agency. The goal of this step isn't productivity. It's proof to your nervous system that you can move. Even the smallest thing counts.
